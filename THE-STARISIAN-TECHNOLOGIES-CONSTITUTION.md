The Starisian Technology Constitution
=====================================

(Canonical, Non-Interpretive Form)
----------------------------------

Status: Foundational law\
Scope: All Starisian Technologies / SPARXSTAR systems, data, workflows, automation, and AI use\
Enforcement: Mandatory via SPARXSTAR platform\
Override: None

* * * * *

Article I --- Human Semantic Authority
------------------------------------

1.  Cultural meaning originates exclusively from human contributors.

2.  No automated system may assign, infer, or finalize semantic meaning.

3.  Automation is limited to structural, syntactic, and procedural operations.

4.  Any system output implying semantic authority is invalid.

* * * * *

Article II --- Explicit Authority & Non-Silent Power
--------------------------------------------------

1.  All authority must be role-bound, state-bound, and logged.

2.  No actor (human or machine) may modify cultural material without:

-   an explicit role

-   a valid workflow state

-   a recorded action

4.  Silent modification is prohibited.

5.  Unlogged change constitutes constitutional violation.

* * * * *

Article III --- Provenance Invariance
-----------------------------------

1.  Every cultural artifact must retain immutable provenance:

-   originator

-   time

-   place

-   method of capture

3.  Provenance may be appended but never erased.

4.  Loss of provenance invalidates the artifact for:

-   archival use

-   research use

-   AI training use

* * * * *

Article IV --- Consent as a Temporal State
----------------------------------------

1.  Consent is contextual, temporal, and revocable.

2.  Consent must be evaluated at every irreversible transition.

3.  Consent state is inseparable from workflow state.

4.  Data without valid consent may not progress.

* * * * *

Article V --- Workflow Legitimacy
-------------------------------

1.  All actions must occur as valid workflow transitions.

2.  Field-level validity is insufficient without workflow legitimacy.

3.  Illegitimate transitions are rejected regardless of data correctness.

4.  Workflow state is the primary system truth.

* * * * *

Article VI --- Separation of Powers
---------------------------------

1.  Contributors may provide content but may not define structure.

2.  Reviewers may assess quality but may not alter provenance.

3.  Administrators may govern state but may not rewrite meaning.

4.  Automation may enforce rules but may not exercise judgment.

* * * * *

Article VII --- Constraint Realism
--------------------------------

1.  The system must assume:

-   intermittent connectivity

-   shared devices

-   oral-first contribution

3.  Designs that require stable broadband or individual devices are invalid.

4.  Audio is a primary epistemic form, not a derivative asset.

* * * * *

Article VIII --- Offline Non-Authority
------------------------------------

1.  Offline actions are provisional by definition.

2.  Offline submissions must be explicitly marked tentative.

3.  No offline action may produce irreversible state change.

4.  Authority is conferred only after server-side validation.

* * * * *

Article IX --- Durability Over Convenience
----------------------------------------

1.  Long-term interpretability supersedes short-term usability.

2.  Performance is measured by certainty, not speed.

3.  Ambiguous success states are prohibited.

4.  Slow but explicit systems are preferred to fast but uncertain ones.

* * * * *

Article X --- Silence as Signal
-----------------------------

1.  Absence of contribution is a first-class datum.

2.  Decline, stagnation, and silence must be tracked.

3.  Growth-only metrics are constitutionally insufficient.

4.  Suppressing silence indicators constitutes data distortion.

* * * * *

Article XI --- Automation Boundary
--------------------------------

1.  Automation may:

-   validate structure

-   flag violations

-   enforce process

3.  Automation may not:

-   approve meaning

-   canonize content

-   replace human authority

5.  AI output is advisory unless explicitly ratified by a human role.

* * * * *

Article XII --- Irreversibility Threshold
---------------------------------------

1.  Any action that renders data "official" must be:

-   deliberate

-   visible

-   logged

3.  Irreversible transitions require human acknowledgment.

4.  Accidental canonization is unconstitutional.

* * * * *

Article XIII --- Constitutional Supremacy
---------------------------------------

1.  Any feature, workflow, or automation that violates an Article is void.

2.  Convenience, performance, or funding considerations do not justify violation.

3.  Orchestrator exists to enforce this Constitution at runtime.

* * * * *

Canonical Statement (Non-Marketing)
-----------------------------------

The Starisian Technology Constitution defines enforceable limits on authority, automation, consent, and structure to ensure that cultural memory remains human-authored, traceable, and durable under real-world constraints.
